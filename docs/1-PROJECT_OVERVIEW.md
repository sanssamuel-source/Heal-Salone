# HEAL Salone - Project Overview

## 1. Problem Statement

### Simple Version

Sierra Leone faces significant health challenges, including maternal emergencies and disease outbreaks (like Cholera), often worsened by slow reporting and lack of real-time data. Communities need a way to speak up, and authorities need a way to listen and act fast.

### Technical Version

The current public health surveillance infrastructure in Sierra Leone suffers from high latency in data collection, unstructured reporting channels, and a lack of centralized, actionable analytics. This fragmentation leads to delayed response times for critical health events (maternal mortality, infectious disease vectors) and prevents data-driven resource allocation by the Ministry of Health.

By bridging low-tech community reporting (SMS/Web) with high-tech AI analysis, we replace weeks of paper-based delays with sub-second real-time intelligence, empowering the Ministry of Health to stop outbreaks before they become epidemics.

## 2. Solution Overview

**HEAL Salone** (Health Emergency AI Line) is a civic-tech platform designed to democratize health reporting and optimize government response.

### Key Features

- **Crowdsourced Health Reporting:** A simple, intuitive interface (Web & Mobile) for citizens to report symptoms, unsafe water, or localized outbreaks.
- **AI Triage Assistant:** An integrated LLM-powered assistant (OpenAI) that guides users through symptom checking, provides immediate first-aid advice in local context, and categorizes report severity.
- **Real-Time Analytics Dashboard:** A "Mission Control" for the Ministry of Health/Admins to visualize hotspots on a map, track infection trends, and allocate resources efficiently.
- **Local Language Support:** Interface and AI interactions designed to be accessible (English/Krio context).
- **Government Integration:** Exports standardized data formats compatible with existing Ministry of Health DHIS2 systems.
